Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 20, 2025

1. Introduction

DOA ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our Device Optical Aid application and related services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1. Information You Provide

  • Account information (email, password)
  • Vision prescription data
  • Payment information for Premium subscriptions
  • Support communication records
  • User feedback and preferences

2.2. Automatically Collected Information

  • Device information (type, operating system, unique identifiers)
  • Usage statistics and patterns
  • Performance and error data
  • Screen settings and calibration data

3. How We Use Your Information

3.1. Primary Uses

  • Providing and optimizing screen correction services
  • Processing subscription payments
  • Maintaining and improving our Services
  • Responding to support requests
  • Sending service updates and notifications

3.2. Secondary Uses

  • Analyzing usage patterns to improve features
  • Developing new products and services
  • Marketing communications (with consent)
  • Research and development

4. Data Storage and Security

4.1. We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your data.

4.2. Prescription data is encrypted during transmission and storage.

4.3. We retain data only as long as necessary for service provision or legal requirements.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

5.1. We Share Data With:

  • Service providers for payment processing
  • Analytics partners for service improvement
  • Legal authorities when required by law
  • Business partners with your explicit consent

5.2. We Do Not:

  • Sell your personal information
  • Share prescription data with third parties
  • Use data for unauthorized purposes

6. Your Rights and Choices

You have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Correct inaccurate information
  • Delete your account and data
  • Opt-out of marketing communications
  • Export your data
  • Withdraw consent for data processing

7. Children's Privacy

7.1. Users must be 13 or older to use our Services.

7.2. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

7.3. Parents may request deletion of children's data.

8. International Data Transfers

8.1. Your data may be processed in different countries.

8.2. We ensure appropriate safeguards for international transfers.

8.3. We comply with applicable data transfer regulations.

9. Cookies and Tracking

9.1. We use cookies for essential service functionality.

9.2. Users can control cookie preferences through browser settings.

9.3. We honor Do Not Track signals.

10. Changes to This Policy

10.1. We may update this Privacy Policy periodically.

10.2. Users will be notified of material changes.

10.3. Continued use constitutes acceptance of changes.

12. Legal Compliance

We comply with applicable data protection laws, including:

  • General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
  • California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
  • Other applicable regional regulations
